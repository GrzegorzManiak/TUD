The top 5 most frustrating things on the web

What are the most frustrating things that you face on the web today? Vitaly Friedman of Smashing Magazine did a great job summering all key user frustrations in 2020.

  1. 1. Small-sized text

    Despite the recent popularity of vide format, most information on the internet is still in writen form.

    good readability and legibility are essential for good user experience

  2. 2. Tiny click targets

    The smaller the interactive elements (links, buttons, and other UI controls), the more mistakes the user will make when interacting with your website.

    Do not play 'hunt a button' game with your visitors.

  3. 3. Unexcpected content shifts

    You are about to click on the link. you hover a cursor over the link & click it, and you suddenly realize that you're doing a wrong thing. Instead of the link you meant to click, you click on the ad, Familiar, right?

    Content shift typically happens as a result of dynamically loading content. Since this operatiob is asynchtonous, the dynamic content is plopped into the page and shifts existing content.

  4. 4. Data loss on errors

    You spend 10 minutes filling data in a web form and finally click the 'Submit' button. The web page reloads, and yiu see the same web form but without your data. The only new thing on this page is a message colored in red that says "Error."/ You level of frustration maximizes.

    One of the most inportant rules of interactive desgin is simple:

    User data is scared

  5. 5. Not working "Back" button

    Usr control and freedom is one of Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability of user interface design. It says:

    Users need a clearly marked "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted action withoit having to go trough an xtended process.

    And the Back button in a browser is the emergency exit. If you are afraid that the users will lose their data by clicking the Back button, it's better to warn users about that by displaying a message "Your work will be lost" on the Back button click
